Defund the Police

"A new report is shedding light on how elevated crime has taken a toll on mid-size U.S. cities in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic and the 'Defund the Police' movement.

In 2022, Pittsburgh reached its highest number of homicides in decades with 71. The city also saw a 46% increase in shootings from 2020 to 2021.

Tucson, Arizona saw 67 homicides in 2022, and non-fatal gun crimes increased by 19% from 2021 to 2022. Gun violence, drug abuse and homelessness all increased that year as well.

Aggravated assaults nearly doubled in Henderson, Nevada in 2022. Robberies also jumped by 91% during the first seven months of that year.

Atlanta saw 31% more burglaries and 22% more motor vehicle thefts in 2022 than the year prior, according to the report. Aggravated assaults also increased to 3,465 from just 970 the year before.

Between 2020 and 2021, Milwaukee led the nation in car thefts after these crimes increased by 111%. The city saw an overall crime spike of 22.6% during the same period.
ABC 2023

"America's retailers are seeing more losses from theft, which is increasingly taking on more aggressive forms.The National Retail Federation just released its annual security survey, which found so-called 'shrink' accounted for over $112 billion in losses last year. That’s an industry term for measuring inventory losses, which aren’t always a result of theft. Some losses stem from administrative or operational errors, for example. But theft accounts for essentially two-thirds of retail shrink... And such thefts are becoming more aggressive than ever before, said David Johnston, vice president of asset protection and retail operations for the National Retail Federation... '(The) majority of these are not stealing for need,' Johnston said. 'They're stealing for greed, knowing that they can go to ... some form of organized group to resell the merchandise and get it back into the marketplace.'

Target just announced it will close nine locations in four markets next month due to ongoing theft and 'fundamental challenges to operating these stores safely'... The survey found about 45% of retailers reduced some store hours in response to retail crime or violence, and 28% even closed locations... And another 67% reported an increase in repeat offenders in places where officials have reduced or eliminated cash bail."

"Hundreds of Pittsburgh Police officers are eligible to retire today, and no new officers are in the pipeline to replace them. On top of that, the city has seen a dramatic decline in the number of police applicants. The city has seen a dramatic decline in applicants, from more than 2,000 a decade ago to under 300 for each of the past two years.

The city will create a 'Telephone Reporting Unit' (TRU) to dispatch calls that do not require an in-person response by officers...
